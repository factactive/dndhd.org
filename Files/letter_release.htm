Letter to request to release 
24 members of PAPVN

San Jose-California, June 21, 1999

To: Secrectary General, Mr. Le Kha Phieu, and all of the Members to Central Committee of the Vietnamese Communist Party.

Re: Demand for the release of all 21 members of the People Action's Party of Vietnam improperly being detained by the Vietnamese Government without trial for more than 2 years.

The imposition of an outdated law in today's society, the usurpation of political power for corruption and private gains, the use of forces to oppress legitimate and nonviolent political dissidents, and the use of police and military to oppress innocent people generally occur only in totalitarian and dishonest regimes.

You and your party are cause of Vietnam's national woes. You have failed to lead the country on the right direction. You have not served the Vietnamese people as a government of the people, by the people and for the people would do. On the contrary, you have built political machinery in the service of corruption, crime, and evil. Specifically, you maintained a policy of suppressing legitimate political dissidents, and of oppressing citizens who pointed out your corruption and wrongdoing. Peoples of the world have witnessed your deceit and crimes since. You should cease such policies and practices. You should start to tell the truth, do the right things, and start being honest.

Your party's consistent mistreatments The People's Action Party of Vietnam is wrongful from political, moral, and justice viewpoints. For review, in April of 1992 your party has persecuted The People's Action Party of Vietnam, detaining members thereof over 400 days. In November of 1996, you unlawfully arrested 17 members of our Party who reside in Cambodia and imprisoned them in Vietnam over the objection of UN High Commission representatives. In June of 1997, once more time, you secretly arrested another 4 members of our Party who were returning from Cambodia to Vietnam to visist their relatives, including a Buddhist monk, Rev. Thich Nhat Si.

Most recently, in the late February of this year, your secret police detained me for more than 3 days for interrogation. You improperly interrogated me about the activities of The People's Action Party of Vietnam while I was on a medical mission on the invitation of your Department of Health to teach the latest heart treatment techniques and to treat Vietnamese patients who have the emergency heart problems. Hundreds of patients were disappointed with your decision to arrest and detain me. Your decide that maintaining your political and power and privileges is more important than the health of our people or the futures of our country. In this same occasion, some of your party members admitted to me that you have held 21 members of The People's Action Party of Vietnam in Chi Hoa prison as your political prisoners. But to date, you dare not to put them to public trials. The reason is very simple. They have not committed any crimes against anyone. Conversely, our Party is supported by the people, and even some members of your party, with good conscience, have agreed with our Party's positions. For those reasons, you only response is to secretly oppress our Party and deny your actions.

To remind you, oppressing the innocent people is a crime of the government, and is against moral values. Also oppressing nonviolent political dissidents is not appropriate with today's civilized world. A government committed to wrong doing, to ill-advised policy, and which oppress its people is a regime of corruption and the sources of crimes and will be dislodged completely. The people of Vietnam know that you, yourselves are the party, the government, the police, and the court of law ...You have all powers, but sadly you do not have a good conscience which belongs to moral Vietnameses.

Moreover, in today's world, in reality, the problem of maintaining a political monopoly to an individual, a organization or a political party in any country, that is, a crooked imagination, injustice, and only causing crimes. We believe, Mr. Secretary-General may also agree to this notion of the people and the world regardless what type of communist you are.

In conclusion, we urge you to rethink and reevaluate of your policies and practices. You should immediately release 21 members of The People's Action Party of Vietnam (17 were arrested in November of 1996 and 4 members in June of 1997). Your detention of these people over two years without trial, that means you even violate your own law, the so-called provision 31 CP, even though it is a kind of law to oppress the innocent people!

We expect you to stop using action of oppression whenever the people criticize you and your party. Dialogue instead of oppressing. Remember, your policies and practices cause dissension and opposition by the citizens. The people have right as human to present their viewpoints and positions. We hope you will make the right decisions in days ahead.

Truly,

Nguyen Xuan Ngai. MD.
Vice chairman
